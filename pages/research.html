Research Themes

Exploring the universe from the formation of stars to the evolution of galaxies. Our collaboration spans across key areas of modern astrophysics.

Fundamental Cosmology & The Dark Universe

Dark Energy Hubble Tension Testing Gravity

Mapping the expansion of the universe to solve the mysteries of dark energy, dark matter, and the Hubble Tension.

Galaxy Evolution & Formation

Galaxy Evolution IFS Stellar Populations

Investigating how galaxies grow and transform over cosmic time, from the first clumpy seeds to todayâ€™s diverse morphologies.

Dense Stellar Systems & High-Energy Astrophysics

Black Holes Gravitational Waves N-Body Sims

Using high-performance simulations to model the interactions of stars in clusters and the formation of the universe's most exotic compact objects.

Active Galactic Nuclei

AGN Quasar Feedback Radio Galaxies

Examining the life cycles of supermassive black holes and how the energy they release can "quench" or stop star formation in their host galaxies.

Multi-Wavelength Survey Science & Data Analytics

Big Data Machine Learning ASKAP

Processing and analyzing massive datasets from radio to X-ray to build a high-resolution history of the baryonic universe.

Cultural & Indigenous Astronomy

Indigenous Astronomy Science Education

Integrating First Nations celestial knowledge into modern astrophysics research and science education frameworks.

Exoplanetary Science & Habitability

Exoplanets Astrobiology Habitability

Detecting new worlds beyond our solar system and assessing their potential for life by studying their orbits and atmospheric compositions.

Stellar Astrophysics & Space Weather

Asteroseismology Space Weather Stellar Magnetism

Investigating the "internal quakes" and magnetic activity of stars to understand how they influence the planetary systems around them.

Solar System & Orbital Dynamics

Asteroids Dynamics Comets

Modeling the origins and stability of planetary systems, including the tracking and evolution of "small bodies" like asteroids and comets.
