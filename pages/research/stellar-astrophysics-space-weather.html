Research Theme

Stellar Astrophysics & Space Weather

Probing the interiors, magnetism, and activity of stars — from quiet Sun-like dwarfs to flaring young stars — and how that activity drives the space-weather environments of their planets.

Asteroseismology Space Weather Stellar Magnetism Flares Stellar Winds

Overview

Stars are not the steady, idealised lamps of textbook diagrams. Their interiors host complex oscillations and dynamo-driven magnetism, and their atmospheres launch winds and energetic flares that can reshape any orbiting planets.

QUASAR researchers connect interior physics to surface activity to planetary impact, treating host stars as central to understanding their planetary systems.

Key science questions

  • What do oscillation modes reveal about stellar interiors, ages, and rotation?
  • How do magnetic dynamos operate across different stellar masses and evolutionary phases?
  • How do flares, coronal mass ejections, and winds shape planetary atmospheres?
  • What can the Sun teach us about other stars, and vice versa?
  • How can stellar activity be modelled and removed when searching for exoplanets?

Approach & methods

  • Asteroseismic analysis of high-precision photometric time series.
  • Spectropolarimetry to map stellar magnetic fields.
  • Magnetohydrodynamic modelling of stellar atmospheres and winds.
  • Flare and variability statistics across large stellar samples.
  • Solar-stellar comparisons leveraging high-cadence Sun-as-a-star observations.
