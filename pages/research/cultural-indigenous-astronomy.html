Research Theme

Cultural & Indigenous Astronomy

Recognising First Nations peoples as the world's first astronomers, this theme supports respectful, community-led collaboration that brings Indigenous sky knowledge into dialogue with contemporary astronomical research and science education.

Indigenous Astronomy Science Education Community-Led Outreach

Acknowledgement

QUASAR acknowledges the Traditional Owners of the lands across Queensland on which our observatories, campuses, and research are situated, and pays respect to Elders past and present. Indigenous knowledge of the night sky is held by communities, and any work in this theme is guided by the consent, leadership, and protocols of the relevant Knowledge Holders.

Overview

Indigenous astronomical traditions encode careful, long-term observations of the sky — of seasonal markers, navigation, weather, ecology, and cultural practice. They sit alongside contemporary astrophysics as distinct, sophisticated bodies of knowledge.

QUASAR aims to support partnerships in which both ways of knowing are respected, with research and education benefits flowing back to Indigenous communities.

Focus areas

  • Co-designing research and outreach with Traditional Owners and Knowledge Holders.
  • Embedding First Nations astronomy meaningfully in school and university curricula.
  • Supporting Indigenous students and researchers across the QUASAR member institutions.
  • Public programs that centre Indigenous voices and storytelling.
  • Ethical frameworks for cultural knowledge in academic publishing and data sharing.

Approach

Activities under this theme will be developed in partnership with Indigenous communities and organisations, following community-led protocols. Detail on specific projects will be added only with the consent and direction of those partners.
