Research Theme

Active Galactic Nuclei

Probing the life cycles of supermassive black holes — how they grow by accretion, launch relativistic jets, and inject energy into their host galaxies, regulating star formation across cosmic time.

AGN Quasar Feedback Radio Galaxies Accretion Jets

Overview

Active galactic nuclei are among the most luminous persistent sources in the universe, powered by accretion onto supermassive black holes at the centres of galaxies. Their radiation, winds, and relativistic jets can outshine their host galaxies and reach far into the surrounding intergalactic medium.

Understanding AGN is essential for galaxy evolution: feedback from accreting black holes appears to shape the upper end of the galaxy mass function and the thermodynamics of cluster-scale gas.

Key science questions

  • How is energy released and channelled in accretion flows around supermassive black holes?
  • How does AGN feedback couple to interstellar and circumgalactic gas?
  • What launches and collimates relativistic jets, and how do they evolve through host environments?
  • How do AGN luminosity functions and obscured populations evolve across cosmic time?
  • How do supermassive black holes co-evolve with their host galaxies?

Approach & methods

  • Multi-wavelength imaging and spectroscopy from radio to X-ray.
  • Reverberation mapping and variability studies of accretion flows.
  • Radio interferometry of jets, lobes, and host environments.
  • Coupled black-hole/galaxy evolution simulations.
  • Statistical analysis of large AGN samples from wide-field surveys.
