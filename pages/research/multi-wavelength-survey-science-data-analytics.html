Research Theme

Multi-Wavelength Survey Science & Data Analytics

Building a high-resolution history of the baryonic universe by processing and combining datasets that span the electromagnetic spectrum — from low-frequency radio surveys to high-energy X-ray imaging — with scalable, statistically rigorous analytics.

Big Data Machine Learning ASKAP Cross-Matching Transients

Overview

Modern astronomy is increasingly data-driven. Wide-field surveys now produce catalogues of billions of sources and image streams measured in petabytes per year. Each waveband reveals different physics, and the most powerful insights come from combining them.

QUASAR groups develop the pipelines, statistical methods, and machine-learning tools needed to extract robust science from these datasets while controlling systematics and uncovering rare phenomena.

Key science questions

  • How can we efficiently classify and characterize billions of astronomical sources?
  • What can multi-band observations tell us about source physics that single bands cannot?
  • How do we detect rare and transient phenomena in high-volume data streams?
  • How do we propagate uncertainties cleanly through joint, cross-matched datasets?
  • Where can machine learning genuinely accelerate discovery, and where does it risk hiding systematics?

Approach & methods

  • Pipeline development for radio (e.g. ASKAP) and optical/infrared surveys.
  • Probabilistic cross-matching across catalogues with different resolution and depth.
  • Machine learning for classification, photometric redshifts, and anomaly detection.
  • Real-time alert handling and rapid follow-up of transients.
  • Bayesian and likelihood-free inference on population-level science questions.
