Research Theme

Solar System & Orbital Dynamics

Modelling the origins, architecture, and long-term stability of our Solar System — including the small bodies (asteroids, comets, and trans-Neptunian objects) whose orbits encode the history of planetary formation and migration.

Asteroids Dynamics Comets Planetary Defence N-Body

Overview

Small bodies are the fossils of planet formation. Their orbital distributions and surface properties record the dynamical history of the Solar System, including planetary migrations and scattering events that shaped the configuration we see today.

QUASAR researchers combine wide-field surveys, targeted observations, and N-body simulations to reconstruct that history and to track the small populations that pose long-term impact risk to Earth.

Key science questions

  • How did the architecture of the Solar System form, and how stable is it on long timescales?
  • What do asteroids, comets, and trans-Neptunian objects tell us about early Solar System chemistry?
  • How do resonances and chaotic dynamics shape small-body populations?
  • How do we identify and track potentially hazardous near-Earth objects?
  • What lessons does our Solar System hold for the architectures of exoplanet systems?

Approach & methods

  • Long-baseline orbital integration and N-body simulations.
  • Photometric and spectroscopic characterisation of small bodies.
  • Synergies with wide-field surveys for discovery and recovery.
  • Dynamical population modelling and resonance analysis.
  • Risk assessment and monitoring frameworks for near-Earth objects.
